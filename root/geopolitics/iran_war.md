# On the Iran War

If there's one word to describe the Iran War, it is *tragic*.

Many will call the US and Israel as an aggressor, and while that is certainly true in the immediate context of the war; it becomes murky when you factor in the history of the conflict and Iran's role.

The fatal mistake that Iran did, in my opinion, was to:

- consider the nuclear program a "bargaining chip", than commit to the end
- fund the 3 Hs: Hezbollah, Hamas and Houthis

The Iranians have been making a nuclear weapon for well over 30 years now. While many sources claim that Iran has achieved a very high level of uranium enrichment; there is still a fair bit of engineering left to come up with a nuclear arsenal, such as the actual construction of the bomb (not as complicated) and miniaturization (complicated) of warheads to be delivered by missiles than fixed wing aircraft.

If you are going to make a nuclear bomb, you have to be low profile about it. Screaming death to America and death to Israel did not inspire confidence.

Moreover, the resources that Iran should have used into developing its arms industry went into funding groups like Hezbollah and Hamas. That were a nuisance for Iran's enemies at best, and a massive liability for Iran at worst.

As we saw post October 7th, the Israelis responded to the gruesome attacks by unrestrained force, decimating Hezbollah and Hamas. In the process: Ba'athist Syria fell, much of Hezbollah and Hamas leadership eliminated, as well as top leadership of the Houthis.

We should not see the Syria War, the Gaza War, the war against Hezbollah, and Houthis, and the Iran War as separate entities. These are the same war and will be referred so in the coming decades, '**The Great Middle Eastern War of 2023-202X**', it shall be called so.

Iran had a choice to make: to choose to fight Israel, or to retrench its position and pursue closer ties with China and Russia. The issue is, there's an **OR** here. China and Russia are not irrational actors. They will not support a country that calls to annihilate Israel. Russia itself, despite to the dismay of many Global Southists online, has been the *largest supplier of fuel to Israel*. China remains Israel's top goods supplier. 

Iran instead chose the first option. The public support of the Muslim world is a very enticing thing to take up but the issue is: public support itself means nothing in strategic terms. Much of the Muslim world is ruled by autocracies. Autocracies that are happy to take the American dollar and live in power and peace. Public opinion counts for nothing. 

Pakistan, the actual Islamic nuclear power, remains a steadfast American ally and its leadership has regularly conveyed to the US that it wants to cooperate on the Gaza issue. The end result of this courting of the United States is that Pakistan continues to receive Chinese aid and military equipment; because the Chinese, despite the image that they are fiercely anti-American, are willing to deal with a US ally far more than a country hostile to the US. Ultimately, China wants a "G2" - Group of Two, where the US and China together rule the world as partners. Iran is anti-thetical to G2. Pakistan is the posterboy of G2, a Chinese and American ally as a result of its hostility with the larger middle power that is India.

All we can pray for now is that civilian casualties occur as little as possible and the war gets over quickly.
